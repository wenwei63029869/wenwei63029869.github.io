Changing Blog Pages

By Wei Wen September 24, 2015.

After 8 weeks of blogging, I decided to completely change the outline of the blog page. Here are a few feedback I have gethered:

  • . - Background color is too deep, which makes it hard to read content.
  • * - The page outline looks simple but not efficent.
  • + - No links leading to social media and Github.
  • \d - No links to make traveling between homepage and blogs easier

As a result, I made changes accordingly.

Firstly, I change backgroud to white and the font-color for most contents to be black. Therefore, it is more readable for viewers now.

Secondly, I made the outline a little bit more complicated but more efficient. For example, I made the width of body content narrower. In that case, people's eyeballs won't need to travel too long distance to look at different sections. Also, I divided all the blogs into two categories - technical and culture blogs. Therefore, it will be easier for viewers to identify the type of blog contents.

In addition, I put a navigation bar at the top, so viewer can easily travel from blog pages to homepage and other pages. Also, I added a side bar which includes newest updated blogs. Therefore, viewers can go straight to the newest blogs directly. At the bottom, I added a navigation bar which includes social media links. In the future, I will make these links for sharing the blogs on different social media platfroms.
