Abstract Sequential

By Wei Wen on August 8, 2016.

My thinking style is abstract sequential. According to Anthony Gregorc, a phenomenological researcher, lecturer, consultant, author and President of Gregorc Associates, Inc.:

    Abstract sequential learner likes:

  • his/her point to be heard
  • analyzing situations before making a decision or acting
  • applying logic in solving or finding solutions to problems

    • They learn best when:

  • they have access to experts or references
  • placed in stimulating environments
  • able to work alone

    • What's hard for them?

  • Being forced to work with those of differing views
  • Too little time to deal with a subject thoroughly
  • Repeating the same tasks over and over
  • Lots of specific rules and regulations
  • "sentimental" thinking
  • Expressing their emotions
  • Being diplomatic when convincing others
  • Not monopolizing a conversation

I think this description is very accurate. I do have had some of the difficulties mentioned above. When it comes to a pairing session, sometimes I feel like getting straight into the solution if I know how to solve the problem. It frustrates me sometimes that I have to wait for my teammate to fully understand the problem and approach. Therefore, I might try to dictate the conversation and rush to the solution directly without listening to my partner’s opinion. However, I realize that very often my partner could give some very good insights. For example, when I was pairing with another person in week 1. He taught me couple of comment line tricks that are very useful to save time. Therefore, from now on, I will force myself to always ask my partner’s opinions after I express mine.

I do find that with step-by-step instruction and experts, I can learn so much better. Therefore, I will set a goal to myself: I will attend office hours at least once per week.
